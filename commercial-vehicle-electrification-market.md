<p><p>Commercial vehicle electrification refers to the process of replacing conventional internal combustion engine vehicles with electric vehicles (EVs) in industries such as transportation, logistics, and delivery services. Firstly, stringent government regulations regarding carbon emissions and the need to reduce greenhouse gas emissions have led to increased adoption of electric vehicles in commercial fleets. Electric vehicles offer reduced noise pollution, lower operating costs, and improved energy efficiency compared to conventional vehicles. This has attracted the interest of fleet operators looking to reduce their carbon footprint and operating expenses.</p><p>The market for commercial vehicle electrification is also benefiting from technological advancements, such as the development of more efficient batteries and charging infrastructure networks.
