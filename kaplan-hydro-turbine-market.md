<p><p>The Kaplan hydro turbine is a type of propeller turbine used in hydroelectric power plants to convert the energy of flowing water into mechanical energy that can generate electricity. It is named after Viktor Kaplan, an Austrian engineer who developed the design in the early 20th century.</p><p>The Kaplan hydro turbine is known for its versatility and efficiency, particularly in low-head installations where there is a large flow of water but a small hydraulic head. It is widely used in rivers, canals, and tidal areas around the world. The turbine consists of a rotor with adjustable blades that can be angled to optimize efficiency at different flow rates.</p><p>The Kaplan hydro turbine market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years due to increasing demand for renewable energy sources and the need to reduce carbon emissions.
