<p><p>Neuroprotection refers to the prevention or reduction of damage to the nervous system. Various types of neuroprotection strategies exist in the market. Free anti-inflammatory agents work by reducing inflammation, which can cause damage to neurons. Radical trapping agents or antioxidants neutralize harmful radicals that can harm nerve cells. Apoptosis inhibitors prevent programmed cell death, minimizing neuronal damage. Glutamate antagonists reduce excessive glutamate, reducing neuronal excitotoxicity. Neurotrophic factors promote the survival and growth of neurons. Stimulants enhance brain activity. Metal ion chelators remove toxic ions from neurons. Other neuroprotective strategies may include gene therapy, stem cell therapy, and drug delivery systems.</p></p>
