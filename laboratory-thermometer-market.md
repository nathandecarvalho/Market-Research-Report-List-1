<p><p>The laboratory thermometer market is divided into two main types: metal and glass thermometers. Metal thermometers are typically made of stainless steel or nickel and are used for high-temperature applications. They are known for their durability and resistance to corrosion. Glass thermometers, on the other hand, are made of glass and contain a colored liquid (often mercury or alcohol) that expands or contracts with temperature changes. They are commonly used in laboratories due to their accuracy and ease of reading. Both metal and glass thermometers cater to the specific needs and preferences of the laboratory industry.</p></p>
