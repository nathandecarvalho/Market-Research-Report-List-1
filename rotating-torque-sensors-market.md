<p><p>Rotating torque sensors are devices used to measure the torque or twisting force in rotating systems. The market for rotating torque sensors can be categorized into two types: contact-based sensing and noncontact-based sensing. Contact-based sensing involves physical contact between the sensor and the rotating object, allowing for precise and accurate torque measurements. On the other hand, noncontact-based sensing utilizes technology such as magnetic or optical sensors to measure torque without direct physical contact. This approach offers advantages like reduced wear and tear, higher durability, and the ability to measure torque in high-speed applications.</p></p>
