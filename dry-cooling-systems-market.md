<p><ul><li>Air Cooled Condensers (ACCs)</li><li>Air Cooled Heat Exchangers (ACHEs)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dry cooling systems, such as Air Cooled Condensers (ACCs) and Air Cooled Heat Exchangers (ACHEs), are used in power plants to cool down the steam that has been used to generate electricity. ACCs transfer the heat from the steam to the air using a series of finned tubes and fans, while ACHEs transfer heat from a process fluid to the ambient air. These systems eliminate the need for water consumption, making them ideal for areas where water scarcity is a concern.</p></p>
