<p><p>Complete Blood Count (CBC) is a common blood test that provides valuable information about a person's overall health. It consists of three main components: White Blood Cell (WBC) count, Red Blood Cell (RBC) count, and Platelet count. WBC count measures the number of white blood cells present in the blood, which helps detect infections or immune system disorders. RBC count determines the number of red blood cells, aiding in assessing oxygen-carrying capacity and diagnosing anemia. Platelet count checks the formation of blood clots, helping monitor conditions like bleeding disorders or bone marrow diseases.</p></p>
