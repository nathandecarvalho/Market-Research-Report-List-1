<p><p>The solar roofing market includes different types of systems based on their connectivity to the electric grid. On-grid solar rooftops are directly connected to the electrical grid, allowing excess energy to be sold back to the grid. Off-grid solar rooftops are standalone systems with no connection to the grid, providing power in remote locations. Hybrid solar rooftops combine the features of both on-grid and off-grid systems, allowing for grid-connected operation with a backup battery storage system. Grid-interactive solar rooftops enable bidirectional power flow, meaning they can both draw power from and supply power to the electrical grid.</p></p>
