<p><p>Cough suppressant drugs are medications used to alleviate cough symptoms. There are two main types of cough: dry cough and wet cough. Dry cough is characterized by a non-productive cough, where no phlegm or mucus is produced. Wet cough, on the other hand, is characterized by a productive cough with phlegm or mucus being expelled. Cough suppressant drugs for dry cough aim to reduce the urge to cough, while those for wet cough aim to loosen and facilitate the removal of phlegm.</p></p>
