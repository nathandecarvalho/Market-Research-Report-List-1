<p><p>Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) is an autoimmune disease that affects the joints, causing chronic inflammation, pain, and stiffness. Early and accurate diagnosis of RA is crucial to begin timely treatment and prevent further joint damage. There are several diagnostic tests available that aid in the accurate diagnosis of RA.</p><p>The most common diagnostic test for RA is a blood test that measures the presence of specific antibodies, such as rheumatoid factor (RF) and anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide (anti-CCP). Presence of these antibodies in the blood is indicative of RA. Another commonly used test is the erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) test which measures the rate at which red blood cells settle in a test tube, indicating inflammation in the body.</p><p>The market for RA diagnosis tests is expected to grow significantly in the coming years. Factors such as the rising prevalence of RA, advancements in diagnostic technologies, and increasing awareness about early diagnosis and treatment of the disease are driving the market growth. Additionally, the development of novel biomarkers and non-invasive diagnostic techniques is further propelling the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the introduction of point-of-care (POC) testing for RA diagnosis is also contributing to the market expansion.
