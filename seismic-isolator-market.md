<p><p>Seismic isolators are devices used to protect buildings and structures from the destructive effects of earthquakes. They act as shock absorbers by isolating the structure from the ground motion and dissipating the energy generated during an earthquake. Seismic isolators are typically made of rubber, steel, lead, or a combination of these materials and are strategically placed between the foundation and superstructure of a building.</p><p>The global seismic isolator market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years. The increasing frequency and intensity of earthquakes, coupled with growing concerns for the safety of buildings and infrastructure, are driving the demand for seismic isolators. Additionally, stringent government regulations and building codes related to seismic safety are further fueling market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, rapid urbanization and the construction of high-rise buildings in earthquake-prone regions are contributing to the market expansion. <p><strong>The Seismic Isolator Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>NRB</li><li>LRB</li><li>HDR</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The seismic isolator market consists of several types, including NRB, LRB, HDR, and others. NRB (Natural Rubber Bearing) isolators utilize natural rubber to provide damping and isolation during seismic events. LRB (Lead Rubber Bearing) isolators consist of alternate layers of rubber and steel, with a lead core to dissipate energy. HDR (High Damping Rubber) isolators employ a high damping rubber compound for enhanced energy dissipation. Other types of isolators may include friction pendulum systems or spherical bearings, each offering unique features to withstand seismic forces and protect structures from damage.</p></p>
