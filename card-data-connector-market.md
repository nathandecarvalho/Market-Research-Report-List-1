<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The Card Data Connector market is classified into various types based on the method used for connecting cards. </p><p>1. Plug-In: This type involves inserting the card into a compatible socket. It provides a secure and reliable connection, often used in applications where frequent card insertion and removal is required.</p><p>2. Push-Pull: These connectors feature a mechanism that allows the user to push or pull the card for insertion or removal. They offer a quick and easy connection method, commonly used in consumer electronics and industrial applications.</p><p>3. Bayonet: Bayonet connectors have a locking mechanism that requires a twisting motion to secure or release the card. They provide a secure and stable connection, commonly found in military and aerospace applications.</p><p>4. Crimp: This connector type involves using a crimp tool to attach the connector pins to the card. It offers a cost-effective solution and is commonly used in automotive and industrial applications.</p><p>5. Other: This category includes various types of connectors that do not fit into the above classifications. It encompasses unique and specialized connectors designed for specific applications, offering customization options for specific requirements.</p></p>
