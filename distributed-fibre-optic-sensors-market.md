<p><strong>The Distributed Fibre Optic Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Rayleigh Scattering Based Distributed Sensor</li><li>Brillouin Scattering Based Sensor</li><li>Raman Scattering Based Sensor</li><li>Interferometric Distributed Optical-Fibre Sensor</li><li>Distributed Fibre Bragg Grating Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Distributed fibre optic sensors use optical fibres to measure and monitor various physical parameters along their length. Rayleigh scattering-based sensors detect changes in light scattering caused by external disturbances. Brillouin scattering-based sensors measure temperature or strain by analyzing the scattering of light due to acoustic vibrations. Raman scattering-based sensors analyze the spectrum of light scattered by the fibre to determine temperature or strain. Interferometric distributed sensors use light interference to detect changes in the fibre. Distributed fibre Bragg grating sensors use the reflection of light from periodic gratings embedded in the fibre to measure strain or temperature.</p></p>
