<p><p>Intravenous catheters are medical devices used to administer fluids, medications, or to withdraw blood directly into the veins. These catheters are primarily used in hospitals, clinics, and other healthcare settings for various therapeutic and diagnostic purposes. They are available in different sizes and materials such as polyvinyl chloride (PVC), polyurethane, and silicone. </p><p>The global market for intravenous catheters has been experiencing steady growth over the years. Factors contributing to market growth include the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases such as cancer and diabetes, the rising global geriatric population, and the growing demand for advanced healthcare facilities. Additionally, the rising number of surgical procedures and the need for intravenous administration of drugs and fluids in emergency situations further drive the demand for intravenous catheters.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology have led to the development of innovative intravenous catheters with improved safety features, longer indwelling times, and reduced risk of complications. For instance, the introduction of antimicrobial-coated catheters has significantly reduced the incidence of catheter-related infections.</p><p>In terms of market trends, there is a growing preference for PICC (peripherally inserted central catheter) over conventional peripheral catheters due to their ease of insertion, reduced risk of complications, and longer indwelling times. Moreover, healthcare providers are increasingly adopting multi-lumen catheters, which allow simultaneous infusion of multiple drugs or fluids.</p><p>According to a forecast, the intravenous catheters market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.6% during the forecast period. However, factors such as the high cost of advanced catheters and the risk of catheter-related infections may hinder market growth to some extent.</p></p>

<p><p>The intravenous catheters market consists of various types of catheters used for different purposes. Peripheral catheters are inserted into smaller veins mainly for short-term treatments. Midline peripheral catheters are longer catheters inserted into larger peripheral veins and are used for longer durations. Central venous catheters are inserted into larger central veins near the heart and are used for long-term treatments or for administering medications that may irritate peripheral veins. Each type of catheter serves a different purpose based on the patient's needs and treatment requirements.</p></p>
