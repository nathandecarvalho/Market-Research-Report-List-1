<p><p>The market for cervical cancer drugs can be segmented into three types based on the stage of the disease: pre-malignant lesions, early invasive stage, and advanced invasive stage. Pre-malignant lesions refer to the initial abnormal changes in cervical cells that may develop into cancer. The early invasive stage involves cancer cells that have spread beyond the surface of the cervix but not to distant sites. The advanced invasive stage refers to cancer cells that have spread to nearby tissues and organs or to distant parts of the body. Each stage requires different treatments and drugs.</p></p>
