<p><p>The indwelling peripheral intravenous catheter (PIVC) market consists of three main types: short PIVC, integrated PIVC, and closed PIVC. Short PIVCs are designed for short-term use, typically up to 72 hours. Integrated PIVCs feature additional components like blood sampling ports or extension sets. Closed PIVCs have a valve or other mechanism to prevent blood reflux or contamination and are commonly used in high-risk scenarios. These different types cater to various healthcare needs and provide options for healthcare professionals to choose the most appropriate PIVC for each patient.</p></p>
