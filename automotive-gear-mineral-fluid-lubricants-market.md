<p><p>Automotive Gear Mineral Fluid Lubricants can be categorized into three types based on their chemical composition: Paraffinic Lubricants, Naphtenic Lubricants, and Aromatic Lubricants. Paraffinic lubricants are derived from paraffin wax and are known for their excellent oxidation stability. Naphtenic lubricants are made from naphthenic base oils and are valued for their thermal and chemical stability. Aromatic lubricants, on the other hand, are derived from aromatic hydrocarbons and offer superior solvency and viscosity characteristics. Each type caters to different automotive gear lubrication needs.</p></p>
