The growing consumer preference for natural and healthy ingredients in food products is driving the demand for malted wheat flour. The unique flavor and texture it imparts to these products make it a popular choice among both commercial and home bakers.</p><p>Additionally, malted wheat flour offers several health benefits. <p><p>Malted wheat flour is available in two main types: diastatic and non-diastatic. Diastatic malted wheat flour contains active enzymes that help break down starches into sugars during baking or brewing processes. This type of flour is commonly used for bread making and as a natural sweetener. On the other hand, non-diastatic malted wheat flour does not have active enzymes and is primarily used for its distinct flavor and aroma in baking. Both types of malted wheat flour are popular among food manufacturers and home bakers for their unique qualities and applications.</p></p>
