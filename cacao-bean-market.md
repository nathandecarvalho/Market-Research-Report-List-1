<p><p>The cacao bean market is divided into two main types: Forastero and Criollo. Forastero is the more common variety, accounting for around 80% of the world's cocoa production. It is known for its hardy and disease-resistant nature, producing high yields and a strong chocolate flavor. Criollo, on the other hand, is a rarer and more delicate variety, representing about 5-10% of global cocoa production. Criollo beans possess a softer texture, an aromatic flavor profile, and are often considered more desirable for premium chocolates due to their complex and nuanced taste.</p></p>
