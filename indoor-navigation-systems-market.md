<p><p>Indoor navigation systems are used to navigate and locate objects or individuals within indoor spaces. They employ different technologies, including Ultra-Wideband (UWB), Wi-Fi, Bluetooth Low Energy, RFID, and others. UWB systems offer high accuracy and precision in determining locations. Wi-Fi-based systems use existing network infrastructures for positioning. Bluetooth Low Energy enables low-power communication between devices for indoor tracking. RFID systems use radio frequency identification for location identification. Other technologies may include computer vision or acoustic-based systems. Each technology has its unique advantages and limitations, and the choice depends on the specific requirements of the application.</p></p>
