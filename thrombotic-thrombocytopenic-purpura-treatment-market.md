<p><p>Thrombotic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (TTP) is a rare blood disorder characterized by the formation of blood clots in small blood vessels throughout the body, leading to low levels of platelets and a decrease in red blood cell count. Prompt and effective treatment is necessary to prevent organ damage and potential fatality in patients with TTP.</p><p>The primary treatment for TTP involves plasma exchange therapy, also known as plasmapheresis. This procedure involves removing the patient's blood plasma, which contains the abnormal proteins causing the clotting, and replacing it with healthy donor plasma. This helps to replenish the deficient enzymes responsible for blood clot breakdown, thereby reducing clot formation.
