<p><p>Permanent flame retardant fibers are classified into two main types: staple fiber and long fiber. Staple fibers are short fibers that are typically 1-5 inches in length and are typically used in applications such as protective clothing and upholstery. Long fibers are longer, typically over 5 inches in length, and are used in applications such as automotive textiles and curtains. Both types of fibers undergo a permanent flame retardant treatment during manufacturing, providing enhanced fire resistance properties.</p></p>
