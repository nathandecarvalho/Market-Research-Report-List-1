<p><p>Instrumentation cables are commonly used in industrial settings to transmit signals from instruments to control systems. There are two main types of instrumentation cables: unarmored cable and armored cable. Unarmored cables do not have any protective layers, making them flexible and suitable for installations where mechanical protection is not required. On the other hand, armored cables have a layer of metal or plastic armor, providing additional protection against mechanical stresses, such as crushing or impact. Both types of cables play a crucial role in facilitating accurate and reliable communication between instruments and control systems in various industrial applications.</p></p>
