<p><p>Real-Time Locating Systems (RTLS) are used to track and locate objects or people in real-time. Some commonly used types of RTLS include ZigBee, Wi-Fi, and Ultra Wide Band (UWB) technologies. ZigBee is a low-power wireless communication protocol that enables small-scale tracking applications. Wi-Fi utilizes existing wireless infrastructure to provide indoor localization and tracking capabilities. UWB technology offers high-precision tracking over short distances by using low-power radio waves. These different types of RTLS technologies cater to various tracking requirements in different settings, such as hospitals, warehouses, or manufacturing facilities.</p></p>
